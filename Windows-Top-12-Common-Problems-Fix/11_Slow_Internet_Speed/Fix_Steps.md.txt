# Slow Internet Speed

## Symptoms:
- Web pages load slowly or not at all.
- Streaming services buffer frequently.
- Downloads and uploads take longer than usual.
- Online games experience lag or delays.

## Most Common Fixes:

1. **Restart the Modem and Router**:
   - One of the easiest and most effective solutions is to restart your **modem** and **router**.
   - Simply unplug both the modem and router, wait for 30 seconds to 1 minute, and then plug them back in.
   - This will refresh the network connection and often solves issues related to slow internet speeds.

2. **Check for Background Applications Using Bandwidth**:
   - Many times, other applications on your system might be consuming bandwidth and slowing down your internet connection.
   - Open **Task Manager** by pressing `Ctrl + Shift + Esc`, and go to the **Processes** tab.
   - Look for applications like **streaming services**, **cloud syncing tools** (e.g., **OneDrive**, **Google Drive**), or **update services** that may be using your internet in the background.
   - Right-click on these apps and click **End Task** to stop them temporarily and check if internet speed improves.

3. **Switch to a Wired Connection (Ethernet)**:
   - If you're using **Wi-Fi** and experiencing slow speeds, try switching to a **wired Ethernet connection** if possible.
   - A wired connection offers more stable and faster speeds than Wi-Fi, especially if there is interference from other devices or walls.
   - Simply connect an Ethernet cable from your router to your device (e.g., laptop or PC) and check the internet speed again.

## Fix:
After restarting the **modem** and **router**, the internet speed improved significantly. Closing background applications like **cloud services** and **streaming apps** also helped free up bandwidth. Switching to a **wired connection** improved speed even further, providing more stable and faster internet access.
